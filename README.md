**"By 2027, there will be two types of PMs: Those who are AI-Native, and those who are unemployed."**

Welcome to the largest **AI-Native PM Community in Africa.** A community of African product managers becoming AI-native by building and shipping real AI products, one weekly challenge at a time. This repository is our shared home: the challenge briefs, everyone's submissions, the resource library, and the showcase of what we build.

There is no finish line here, only the next build.

## What This Is

A build-first, ship-always community challenge. Every week a new challenge drops. You build it, deploy it, record a short demo, and submit it. The community reviews it, tears it down, and celebrates it. Then we do it again.

The first twelve weeks are the Foundation Track. After that, the challenges never stop. As new tools, frameworks, and patterns appear, we add new ones. This repo is forever open.

## What You Get, and What You Do Not

There are no certificates here. Nobody hands you a PDF that says you are AI-native. What you walk away with is harder to fake and worth far more:

1. A public portfolio of shipped, deployed AI products that anyone can click and try.
2. A GitHub profile that proves you build, not just talk.
3. Real, current skills across the full AI product stack: prompting, vibe coding, full-stack, agents, MCP, RAG, and evals.
4. Honest peer review from people doing the same work, every single week.
5. A community that pushes you, and members who can vouch for what you have actually built.

## The Foundation Track (Season 1: 12 Weeks)

The track moves from speed of thought-to-product, through connecting to the world, into production-grade AI systems.

### Month 1: The AI-Native Builder

| Week | Theme | What you ship |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | AI-Native PRD and the Logic Engine | A system prompt that turns a raw idea into a valid, reusable JSON spec, with test cases |
| 2 | Vibe Coding and Production UI | A high-fidelity, responsive, deployed interface generated with v0.dev |
| 3 | Design-to-Code Mastery | A working flow built from a Figma AI design through to functional code |
| 4 | The Full-Stack Pivot (Supabase) | A live app with Google auth, a database, and an admin view where you can actually sign up and see your data saved |

### Month 2: Connecting to the World

| Week | Theme | What you ship |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5 | API Intelligence and Data Fetching | A bot that pulls live data via a public API and synthesizes it with function calling (information, not financial or other regulated advice) |
| 6 | CI/CD and Automated Reliability | A GitHub Action that tests on every push and deploys only when tests pass |
| 7 | Building Effective Agents (Tool Use) | A tool-using agent that acts on external systems, with a human approval step on anything consequential |
| 8 | MCP Implementation | A working Model Context Protocol server that connects an AI to real files and tools, with sensible permissions |

### Month 3: The AI Architect

| Week | Theme | What you ship |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 9 | RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) | A knowledge base that answers questions with citations from 100 or more documents |
| 10 | AI Evaluation and Guardrails | An eval suite that measures accuracy, hallucination, and edge cases, run automatically |
| 11 | Multi-Agent Systems | A team of agents that collaborate to produce a real, useful artifact |
| 12 | Capstone: The AI-Native PM Portfolio | A single portfolio hub linking every build, with a video walkthrough of your journey |

Full briefs for each week live in the `challenges/` folder.

## How It Works

Challenges run on a weekly rhythm.

| Day | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| Monday 6AM | New challenge drops with a short walkthrough video |
| Wednesday 8PM | Mid-week standup. Post your blocker, the community helps |
| Saturday 11:59PM | Submission deadline. Repo link plus demo video |
| Sunday 6PM | Live review. We tear down three builds and celebrate three builds |

You do not need paid tools to start. Some later challenges use tools such as Claude Code. Aim to have access by the time you reach them, whether through your company, a personal subscription, or a shared plan with other members.

## Ground Rules: Build Responsibly

We ship fast, and we ship with care:

1. Aim for real problems and real users wherever possible, not mock-only demos.
2. Keep a human in the loop for any consequential or irreversible agent action.
3. Do not ship AI that gives regulated advice, including medical, legal, or financial, without clear disclaimers and visible uncertainty.
4. Respect privacy, secrets, and the terms of any API you use. Never commit keys.
5. Credit your sources and the people whose work you build on.

## ❓ FAQ

### Q: What if I've never coded before?
**A**: The curriculum uses no-code and low-code tools (v0.dev, Lovable, Google Stitch). You'll learn as you go. The community is here to help.

### Q: What if I fall behind?
**A**: You can catch up anytime. The challenge is forever open. There's no deadline. Just keep shipping.

### Q: Can I work in a team?
**A**: Yes! Teams of 2-3 are encouraged. Each person must have their own portfolio repo, but you can collaborate.

### Q: What if my project fails?
**A**: Document what went wrong and why. That's worth more than a successful project with no learnings.

### Q: Do I need to use Google AI Studio?
**A**: No. You can use Claude, Llama, or any model. The curriculum uses Google AI Studio as the default, but you're free to choose.

### Q: What happens after Week 12?
**A**: The challenge continues. New weeks are added as new tools and frameworks emerge. You can keep building, or you can create new challenges.
